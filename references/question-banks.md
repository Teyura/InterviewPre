## 2. Technical Questions — CS / Software Engineering

Generate 4–5 based on their specific stack and target role.

### Core Concepts
- "Walk me through how you would debug a function that isn't returning the expected output."
  - *What they want:* Systematic thinking, not just guessing
  - *Strong structure:* Read the error → isolate the issue → add logs → test assumptions → fix and verify

- "What's the difference between a stack and a queue? When would you use each?"
  - *What they want:* Conceptual clarity + practical application
  - *Strong structure:* Define both → contrast (LIFO vs FIFO) → give one real example each

- "How would you explain REST APIs to someone non-technical?"
  - *What they want:* Communication ability + depth of understanding
  - *Strong structure:* Use an analogy (restaurant order) → explain request/response → practical example

- "Describe a time you optimized a piece of code. What did you do?"
  - *What they want:* Awareness of performance, not just correctness
  - *Strong structure:* STAR format → mention what was slow → what you changed → measurable improvement

- "What version control practices do you follow?"
  - *What they want:* Team readiness, professionalism
  - *Strong structure:* Mention Git → branching strategy → commit message habits → PR/review process

### Sentence starters for technical questions:
- *"My approach to this would be to first..."*
- *"From my experience with [related tool/concept], I would..."*
- *"One way to think about this is..."*
- *"In a project where I encountered something similar, I..."*
- *"I'd start by [action] to confirm my understanding of the problem, then..."*

## 3. Technical Questions — Business / Finance / Accounting

- "Walk me through how you would analyze a company's financial health."
  - *What they want:* Structured thinking, key ratio knowledge
  - *Strong structure:* Revenue trends → profitability (margins) → liquidity (current ratio) → leverage → compare to industry

- "What does EBITDA mean, and why do analysts care about it?"
  - *What they want:* Financial literacy
  - *Strong structure:* Define → why it strips out non-operating factors → when it's useful vs misleading

- "How would you build a financial model for a new product?"
  - *What they want:* Modelling process, assumptions awareness
  - *Strong structure:* Revenue drivers → cost assumptions → scenario analysis → sensitivity

- "Tell me about a time you used data or analysis to support a decision."
  - *What they want:* Analytical mindset, evidence-based thinking
  - *STAR format with specific tool mentioned (Excel, Python, etc.)*

- "What Excel functions do you use most often?"
  - *What they want:* Practical readiness
  - *Strong answer mentions:* VLOOKUP/XLOOKUP, SUMIF, IF nesting, pivot tables, INDEX/MATCH

### Sentence starters:
- *"When analyzing [X], I typically start by..."*
- *"The key assumption I'd want to validate is..."*
- *"From a financial perspective, this matters because..."*

## 4. Technical Questions — Engineering (Non-CS)

- "Walk me through your design process for [type of component relevant to their major]."
  - *Strong structure:* Requirements → constraints → concept generation → analysis → iteration → testing

- "Describe a time you identified a flaw in a design or system."
  - *STAR format; focus on systematic observation, not luck*

- "What safety considerations do you prioritize in your work?"
  - *What they want:* Professional responsibility, process thinking*

- "How do you approach trade-offs between cost, performance, and reliability?"
  - *Strong structure:* Define the constraint hierarchy → how context changes priorities → example*

- "What engineering software/tools are you most comfortable with?"
  - *Name tools specifically (MATLAB, SolidWorks, AutoCAD, ANSYS, etc.) + one concrete project*

## 5. Technical Questions — Science (Bio, Chem, Life Sciences)

- "Describe your experience designing or running an experiment."
  - *Strong structure:* Hypothesis → controls → method → data collection → analysis → conclusion*

- "How do you ensure the accuracy and reproducibility of your data?"
  - *What they want:* Scientific rigor, attention to detail*

- "Tell me about a time your results didn't match your hypothesis. What did you do?"
  - *What they want:* Scientific maturity, ability to pivot*

- "What lab safety protocols do you follow?"
  - *Know the basics: PPE, WHMIS/GHS, waste disposal, emergency procedures*

- "Walk me through how you analyze and present data from an experiment."
  - *Strong answer mentions:* Statistical methods used, visualization choices, interpretation approach*

## 6. Technical Questions — Arts / Communications / Marketing

- "Walk me through a piece of work you're most proud of."
  - *Strong structure:* Brief + Client/Goal + Process + Result + What you learned*

- "How do you approach writing for different audiences?"
  - *What they want:* Audience awareness, adaptability*
  - *Strong structure:* Research the audience → adjust tone/vocabulary/format → test/get feedback*

- "How do you handle feedback on your creative work?"
  - *What they want:* Professionalism, ego management, coachability*

- "Describe how you've used data or metrics to inform a creative decision."
  - *What they want:* Balance of creativity + analytical thinking*

- "Walk me through a campaign or project you contributed to."
  - *STAR format: What was the objective → your role → what you produced → the outcome*

## 7. Motivational Questions (All Programs)

| Question | What they really want | Formula |
|----------|-----------------------|---------|
| "Why do you want to work here?" | Genuine interest + homework done | [Specific company fact] + [Your genuine interest] + [Connection to your goals] |
| "Why should we hire you?" | Confident self-advocacy | [Your top 2 strengths] + [Specific proof] + [Benefit to them] |
| "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?" | Ambition + groundedness | [Short-term goal in this role] + [Longer-term direction] + [Openness to evolve] |
| "What's your biggest weakness?" | Self-awareness + growth mindset | [Real but non-disqualifying weakness] + [What you've done] + [Progress seen] |
| "What do you know about our company?" | Preparation, genuine curiosity | Use Phase 3 company research snapshot |
| "Why did you choose your program?" | Authenticity, direction | Genuine story + connection to career interests |
| "What are your greatest strengths?" | Self-awareness, relevance | 2–3 specific strengths + one proof each |

### Sentence starters for motivational questions:
- *"What really drew me to [company] was..."*
- *"I've been following your work on [specific thing], and..."*
- *"One thing I'm actively working on is..."*
- *"I'm proud to say that..."* (for "why hire you" — give permission to self-advocate)
- *"In five years, I'd hope to..."*
- *"Looking back at why I chose [program], the honest answer is..."*

## 8. Decoded Intentions Quick Reference

Use this when coaching the student on what the interviewer is REALLY testing.

| Surface question | Real evaluation |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "Tell me about yourself" | Are you self-aware? Can you communicate clearly? Are you a fit? |
| "Why us?" | Did you do research? Are you genuinely interested, not just job-hunting? |
| "Biggest weakness?" | Can you reflect honestly? Are you growing? |
| "Team conflict story" | Are you emotionally mature? Can you work with difficult people? |
| "Mistake story" | Do you take responsibility? Do you learn? |
| "Leadership story" | Can you influence others without needing authority? |
| "Challenge story" | Do you stay calm and think clearly under pressure? |
| "Learn quickly story" | Are you adaptable? Do you have a learning system? |
| "Why hire you?" | Can you advocate for yourself? Do you know your own value? |
| "5 years question" | Will you commit? Do you have direction? Will you grow here? |
